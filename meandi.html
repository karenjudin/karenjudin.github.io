Me and I

A photographic exploration of one woman’s relationship with Judaism.

This photo series began as an experiment for me to understand how I view different Jewish women. It should be clarified that these images do not represent women who practice Judaism any more or any less than the other, or any better or any worse than the other. Rather, these images are meant to depict some of the most basic differences that I have seen in Jewish women everywhere, depending on how they choose to practice and observe their religion.

At the beginning of this project, I hoped that I would be able to end the series with a photo of how I define myself as a Jew. But through the process of this experiment I quickly realized that there are parts of myself in all of the women in these images, and there are parts of all of those women in me.
